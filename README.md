A curated resource hub for AI film recap tools, long-video story compression workflows, movie recap structures, and narrative-first clipping notes.

This repository is not built as a single-brand recommendation page. Its purpose is to help creators, editors, and content teams understand a more specific question:

**Which tools are actually useful for film recap, story compression, and narrative-heavy video restructuring, and where do their limits show up?**

## Why Film Recap Is A Different Category

Film recap is not the same thing as podcast clipping or highlight extraction.

Most short-form AI editors are strongest when the source material is:

- speech-heavy
- segment-friendly
- easy to split into quotes or highlights

Film recap is different because it usually depends on:

- narrative continuity
- scene selection
- emotional pacing
- story compression
- voiceover or rewritten structure

That is why many generic AI clipping tools can help with parts of the workflow but still fail to produce a strong recap on their own.

The important point is not that one tool wins every use case. It is that **film recap is its own workflow category**.

## A More Useful Selection Framework

Before picking a tool, answer these five questions:

1. Is the source primarily narrative or primarily spoken?
2. Does the recap need a rewritten script or only a shorter cut?
3. Are you compressing one story or generating multiple clips?
4. Is the priority speed, control, or narrative coherence?
5. Will the final output use voiceover, subtitles, or direct dialogue?

If those questions are not clear, most tool comparisons become shallow.

## Typical Film Recap Workflow

1. Identify the narrative spine
2. Reduce scenes to only story-carrying moments
3. Decide what must be narrated versus shown
4. Rewrite the recap structure for short-form pacing
5. Build subtitles, TTS, or voiceover layer
6. Polish transitions and packaging

This is why film recap usually needs a different tool stack than standard short-form clipping.
